A few days have a way of feeling longer than they are when there is nothing to do but wait. Vos-Greel had been good enough to mention that the bathhouse would need time to prepare itself for guests, and the group had taken him at his word. In the meantime, the estate settled back into its particular rhythm. Mevanos had returned to his tower on the outer edges of Vvardenfell with the scroll from the Reanimatorium tucked under his arm and the satisfied expression of a man who considered the day well spent. He had left it there and gone to sleep.

When he woke up, it was gone. In its place, sitting on the exact surface where the scroll had been, was a golden plate. On the plate was a piece of cheese. He stared at it for a moment with the particular stillness of someone whose brain is working very hard before the rest of them catches up. Then he threw the cheese and started stomping around the tower looking for whoever had done this with the energy of a man who had very specific feelings about being robbed and was going to express all of them at once. He found nothing, no sign of entry, no sign of exit, nothing that indicated anyone had been there at all beyond the plate and the absence of the scroll. He stood in the middle of his tower and arrived, through the process of elimination, at the only organization capable of this kind of clean and pointed professional statement: the Morag Tong. He was not calmer for knowing, he was simply differently angry. He opened a portal to Sugar Falls with smoke coming off him in the literal sense.

Sugar Falls, at the moment of his arrival, was occupied with other concerns. A Khajiit had come through Reaper's March following the particular kind of rumor that either delivers exactly what it promises or wastes an afternoon entirely. His name was Ra'khadar, a Fighters Guild warrior, and his first impression of Sugar Falls was that, whatever the rumors had suggested, in short, not great. He stood in front of it with the expression of someone revising their expectations. This impression was not improved by what came through the door next. A goblin arrived with a cart, which was old and rickety and would have been concerning on its own terms. What it contained made it considerably more so, potions and drinks packed with the confidence of someone who had never been formally introduced to the concept of adequate padding, and several of them were already broken. The goblin, whose name was Mizzi, was unaware of this fact and conveyed the cheerful energy of someone who had not yet received the relevant information. Ra'khadar brought it to her attention, and they set about trying to address the situation with the limited materials available. Two members of the Sugar Falls staff, Aiden and Jeremy, came out to investigate the commotion and understood the assignment; it was time for more work. The four of them got the product inside, where Liliana was in the kitchen when they came through. She asked about the potions, and Ra'khadar and Mizzi explained. Aiden and Jeremy confirmed the product's quality and inspected it personally. The potions were added to the Sugar Falls inventory, and Mizzi looked extremely pleased with herself. Then the mood immediately soured as Mevanos arrived, trailing smoke, holding a golden plate.

Liliana knew that smoke, so she went outside to investigate with Ra'khadar and Mizzi behind her. Mevanos explained himself with the tightly controlled fury of a man who had decided to be precise rather than loud about his anger, which is somehow more unsettling than the alternative that he had been robbed. This plate had been left in exchange for the cheese, presumably somewhere in the tower, and thrown. Liliana looked at the plate; she had seen it before. Not this exact plate, not this exact moment, but the same calling card in the same form at the same kind of scene, a “house party” if you wanted to put it, hosted by a blood mage named Caedis, and a death that had been arranged. The plate had been there then, too; the cheese had been there then as well, presumably not yet thrown. The connection was clear enough that she said it aloud immediately and then suggested that they should find the others. She called out across the estate in the general direction of wherever people might be. Volg and Mia, who had been in the drug den nearby with the easy comfort of people spending time together without a particular agenda, and Wylarin, who had been in the river, came when called. They were brought up to speed with the connection between the “house party” and the tower robbery. The group discussed options, and the criminal organizations that Sugar Falls had connections to were raised as a possible avenue. Ra'khadar, new to this particular gathering but apparently not to the kind of thinking that gets applied to unusual problems, observed that cheese of this kind had associations with Sheogorath that might be worth considering. The room sat with this for a moment. Before any of these avenues could be pursued, the conversation was interrupted by something only some of them could hear.

A portal was activated in Chronus's corner of the Sugar Falls estate. The illusion on it was specific; you had to know it was there to hear it open. Volg, Mia, and Wylarin knew, and they moved toward it with the wariness of people who have learned that nothing good tends to come from that corner, and the others followed the way people follow when a group moves with shared purpose and no one wants to be the one left behind. What came through was Evening's face, checking the portal was working before committing to it fully. He stepped through and found considerably more people than he had been expecting, which registered on him for a moment before he stepped through. Volg and Mia went forward with a particular quality to reunions between people who have been through thick and thin and then separated for long enough; they don't always know what to do with their hands at first. Volg solved it with a bro handshake and half side hug and let go before it became anything more sentimental than that. Evening then asked who everyone was; they told him, and he told them why he was here: Clan Agalor had reached the point where clearing the collapsed underground section was possible, and they needed help. He could offer food and drinks, and nothing else at the moment. This was received as sufficient by most of the room. Liliana agreed to come on the specific condition that she be the one doing the cooking, which Evening accepted with the ease of someone for whom this was no condition at all. They all dressed for the cold while Ra'khadar gathered building supplies with the foresight of someone who has shown up to construction work before and knows what gets forgotten. The portal opened onto the cold winds of Wrothgar, and they went through.

The mountains received them without sentiment, the cold was specific in the way the mountain cold wraps around them, not just in temperature but weight, the kind that settles into the shoulders immediately and stays there. Evening led them through the entrance, near one of the carts, going through a chest with the practiced ease of someone who knows exactly what should be in it, stood Murgakh. He turned when he heard them, and the welcome he extended was the genuine kind, the sort that doesn't need to announce itself; he simply treated them as people worth having arrived, offered drinks before anyone had asked for anything, and listened to why they were there with the attention of a man who takes what people say seriously. He told them that Yashna had already started on the cave-in without waiting for anyone, which he delivered as a fact rather than a complaint, the way you state something about the weather. Shuvra appeared beside them with the particular quality of someone who moves quietly, not for stealth but out of long habit. She told Evening that Morning, and Shel needed him, and so Evening went while the group stayed with Murgakh.

Inside, the main area had the particular atmosphere of a place managing two things at once and doing its best with both of them. The center held the careful arrangement of Hermaeus Mora worship, and around the edges, crammed into whatever space remained, were tents, thirty to forty Orcs making a life in quarters not designed for it. Turning a difficult situation into something livable through the specific stubbornness of people who have decided to keep going. A few Orc children nearby were fighting each other with great conviction, using sticks they had clearly appointed as swords. Suddenly, the lights flared and went out. The camp went quiet the way it goes quiet when something or someone has arrived. Chronus came in with the energy of someone who knew exactly what his entrance cost the room and had been trying to spend less of it lately, with mixed results. He greeted Murgakh with him receiving it with the cordiality of a man who has made a decision about how he is handling someone that caused massive amounts of trauma. The tension sat between them like furniture nobody wanted to move.

Liliana, with the timing of someone who either reads a room very well or simply needed a reason to get to the kitchen, asked about cooking. Murgakh took her there with the specific gratitude of someone who offered an excuse they were glad to take. As he went, he pointed the group toward a nearby tent on his way out. Inside it, surrounded by a quantity of books that suggested she had found the ones worth saving and was cataloguing them before the significance of what was lost could fully land, sat Lorzub. She had a quill behind her ear that she had clearly forgotten was there. She looked up when she noticed them, put the book down, and introduced herself with a smile that was polite and nervous in equal measure. Chronus recognized her; he introduced everyone with the particular care of someone trying to be useful rather than noticed. After proper introductions were made, Lorzub explained the state of things: the cave-in, the lost collection, and the silence from Hermaeus Mora, the absence of his protection being the thing that sat heaviest and the thing she wanted to keep quiet. She mentioned that Evening had taken it upon himself to keep the clan protected in Mora's place. Volg and Mia registered this as the question of why he hadn't mentioned it sat between them without being said immediately. Some things get asked after the room has settled, specifically Lorzub, who turned to Mevanos and mentioned that Morning would need his help with a specific project. The two of them separated from the group to discuss it.

Chronus, meanwhile, had been getting quieter in the way that precedes something rather than follows it. The reminders were everywhere, not accusatory, simply present, the ordinary texture of a place that had been damaged and was being put back together. Each one landed, and eventually there were enough of them, and he walked away from the conversation and toward the nearest cave-in and hit it with an Apocryphal blast that shook the room and started the rocks moving. Near the cave-in stood Yashna, stocky and broad-handed and already working on the rubble before anyone had officially started, bandaged forearm and all. Ra'khadar moved toward her immediately and put himself between her and whatever might come next from the rocks. Liliana, Mia, and Wylarin moved toward Chronus. They stopped him just before the second blast. He stood in the aftermath of what he had done, looked at the room, and walked away ashamed in the specific way that people walk away when there is nothing else available to them. Yashna, once the dust settled, was understandably angry and refused to be healed. She had the stubbornness of someone who had decided that accepting help is a position to be defended, and she defended it as her life depended on it. Wylarin, Volg, and Mia did not argue the point from the outside; they approached it from the side, the way you approach something that has decided to be immovable, and after a while, she let Wylarin treat it. She did not make it easy, mind you; she only made it possible, which was enough. Afterwards, they worked with the hours going by the way hours go when people are doing something physical and useful together, steadily and without drama, for the first time in a while. Ra'khadar set up support beams with the focus of someone who knows exactly what he is doing with structural materials and would rather do it right than quickly. Liliana moved between the kitchen and the work site, which had the effect of making both better. The work went longer because the food kept coming, and the food kept coming because the work gave it purpose. After a while, some of the clan members drifted over and joined in, the way people join things that are clearly going well and clearly could use another set of hands. By the end, Yashna thanked Wylarin. She said it once, directly, without elaboration, and it landed exactly as much as it needed to.

Morning arrived, and she asked where Chronus had gone, where she was told that he had walked off, so she went to find him. She eventually found him near another cave-in entrance, talking with Volg, standing in the particular way of two people who have arrived at something real in a conversation without quite intending to. She told him that Shel wanted to speak with him alone, to which he agreed and walked to the gazebo at the edge of the mountainside, where Shel was sitting with Shuvra nearby. Shel gave him a hug with the gratefulness of someone who had not been themselves for a long time. She thanked him for the staff, properly, looking at him while she did it, and after they sat together for a while and talked about how each of them was doing, which is a question that means something different when both parties have been through something that the other was present for in complicated ways. At some point during this, Shel noticed something in the grass nearby and pointed it out. A rabbit, white, sitting very still, watching them with the particular attention of something that was not there for the grass. Its eyes were green, not rabbit green, not any animal green, but something reptilian and specific, the kind of eyes that belonged to a different kind of creature entirely. From its mouth came black ichor, slow and deliberate as ink. The moment Shel pointed at it, it ran. By the time anyone else could properly look, it was gone. It left nothing behind except the fact of having been there, which is sometimes the most significant thing anything can leave.
