The Den of Sugar Falls sat quietly beneath a grey and indecisive sky, the kind of sky that promises nothing and therefore the stress of the day is nonexistent. After the particular unpleasantness that had surrounded Caedis, the group had earned their idleness. Bayard, Iris, Wylarin, Mia, and Volg moved through the estate the way water moves in a still pool, present, unhurried, content to simply be. It is worth noting, when observing such moments, how deceptive stillness can be. Still water has no idea what stone is about to break its surface.

The first stone arrived with all the grace of something that had run out of options. A Khajiit came over the waterfall tumbling, suddenly, and entirely without dignity. Her name was Rali, though the rocks at the bottom of the waterfall are uncaring to such things. It was Volg and Mia who heard it first, the particular sound of something going wrong near water. It was them who stepped outside to find her crumpled on the bank, unconscious and worse for wear. They brought her inside without ceremony, to tend to her wounds and figure out exactly what was going on with this mysterious Khajit.

Not far away, unconnected and yet about to become very connected, a Kahjit woman named Liliana walked into Sugar Falls looking for someone who was not there yet. She settled near the fire with the patience of someone who had been searching long enough to know that rest was not unwelcome. She watched Iris and Wylarin dance, and let herself be still for just a moment. As she did the meat she put on the fire crackled to perfection as she wondered about the spices that could be used to enhance its flavors. Suddenly a portal announced itself with all the self-importance of magic that has decided it would like an audience. Through it stepped a Dunmer who carried the particular energy of someone who had heard rumors about a place and arrived to determine whether the rumors were worth believing. His name was Mevanos, and he had the look of a man whose stone cold appearance could kill. What Mevanos had not considered, or perhaps had simply not noticed, was that he had brought a passenger. From behind him emerged a figure best described as an old woman that one could best describe as a Reach Witch but not quite. Granny Lexy appeared behind him as though she had always been there and the portal had simply formalized the arrangement.

Meanwhile, in the other wing of the estate, Bayard had heard of the commotion surrounding the Khajiit and went to see about it. Mia and Volg had gone to collect Iris and Wylarin, because wounds, like most problems, improve with more hands plus they both have had a history with helping those with troubled pasts, leaving Bayard to evaluate the situation alone. He healed what he could, which was considerable, and Rali regained enough of herself to immediately demonstrate how she had survived by leaping away from him in pure terror. Mia heard this and moved quickly, arriving with the calm efficiency of someone who has dealt with panic before and pulling from the quieter parts of her vampire nature smoothed out the sharp edges of Rali's fear. It worked, it also, as it turned out, was precisely the kind of help that helps maybe a bit too much. Iris felt the panic before she arrived at it and when she came through the door with Wylarin, Mevanos, and Granny Lexy trailing behind her she took in the scene with a particular expression. She had opinions about what Mia had done, she voiced them, concerned about the magical effect her vampire nature could cause. Volg, for his part, had stayed behind. Not from disinterest but from the awareness of someone who knew his limitations for situations such as these. Liliana was near the fire and Volg was the kind of creature who notices other creatures of his particular nature the way iron notices a magnet. They assessed each other with the silent language of two people who understand that the line between ally and threat can be drawn very quickly. They reached somewhat of an understanding, as they walked over to join the rest. 

Iris set about the work of genuine calming, with the precision of someone paying attention that the architecture of Rali's fear had a specific load-bearing wall in it: Mevanos. Iris asked him, with the careful politeness of someone making a request that was not entirely a request, to wait outside. The moment Liliana saw Mevanos walk outside properly she asked if he recognized her, it was clear he did. Nearly twenty years is a long time to go without seeing someone. It is also, as it happens, long enough for a punch to the chest to feel entirely deserved. She furiously explained that the punch was for abandoning them all those years ago. Liliana delivered it and then, without waiting for him to recover, pulled him into an embrace that said more than the punch had. Some reunions are complicated, this one wore its complications openly. They knew each other, that much was obvious, the specifics of how and why belonged to a longer story that the afternoon was not yet ready to tell. Hours settled around the group the way hours do when people are cautiously becoming acquainted, a little at a time. Names were exchanged, small truths were offered, the kind people share when they are deciding how much of themselves to trust a stranger with. Rali was improving in the specific way that good food and decent healing tend to produce, fortified by the cake that Mia had made and the combined attention of Wylarin and Bayard. She was still watchful but she was present, which was progress. 

Suddenly the overcast very quickly evolved into a full on storm. The storm that rolled in came with the weight of something that had been building since before anyone thought to check the horizon, and with it came two things riding the same wind but belonging to entirely different worlds. The first was the residual shadow of the Planemeld, that old wound in the fabric of things that Solstice still carried in its bones like an ache that never fully resolved. It arrived high into the sky with a hue of purple and blue that seemed to dance its way across the sky. Would have been considered beautiful if not for the implications of its creation. The second was something older and stranger and harder to name: it showed itself as a gust of wind that was not quite just wind, carrying leaves, grass, and the suggestion of intent as it moved through the entirety of the Sugar Falls estate with a hurried rush of something panicked. Then, almost like it finally noticed them, stopped in front of the group. The leaves and grass gathered themselves to the floor with the slow deliberateness of a thought becoming a sentence. The wind shaped itself, and there, assembled from borrowed material and the particular desperation of someone who has tried every other option, stood the visage of an Argonian. It quickly became clear, he was panicking, and when he finally noticed them he was surprised. Both of those things became true simply because his plan had actually worked when he had not expected it to.

He introduced himself and said that his name was Vos-Greel. He explained that he was currently indisposed inside a manor on Solstice and had sent this message the way one throws something into the dark hoping it lands somewhere useful, on the slim possibility that someone was listening. He then asked who they were and where they were located trying to gauge just who exactly he contacted. The group, being reasonable people with appropriate caution, hesitated. Unfortunately for everyone else Granny Lexy did not hesitate. She listed everyone's names with the cheerful completeness of someone who believed information is meant to be shared, mentioned that they were in Reaper's March, and finished before anyone had quite managed to stop her. Vos absorbed this with the gratitude of a man who has been alone long enough to find information from a stranger deeply welcome. He asked for their help as he explained his situation: a manor and an inability to leave. He thought had a way out, he believed, but it required people who were not stuck to help him find it. Bayard and Wylarin were willing to help on the basis of simple decency. Volg however was willing on the basis of compensation. The stumble that followed, in which Vos searched for what he could actually offer them, resolved into a free bathhouse trip for all of them. This landed with Iris and Rali almost immediately. Then Vos, apparently deciding that he should give everything he had to offer, added that the manor was of House Corelanya and contained rare artifacts that could be of value. Mevanos immediately looked up as the group deliberated. Rali, during this deliberation, located what she apparently believed was a weapon in the form of a broom, which she did not immediately correct when she noticed. The group accepted the deal and Mevanos offered to handle the issue of transportation via a portal.

Once there Corelanya Manor arrived in the way that places with reputations tend to have, before you could see it properly, you could feel it. The fog was specific, the stillness was specific, this was not the ordinary absence of wind and sound but the presence of something that had decided, long ago, that they preferred to stay away. Vos was in the doorway as he waved at the group hoping that they would notice him. As they met up he started to explain his situation and he then offered to show them what would happen when he tried to leave the manor. The demonstration of the curse had a quality that unsettles the mind more than straightforward danger does, not pain or threat but wrongness. He tried to walk through the door and then, as though he had never left the doorway, was immediately back in the house, back facing the group. The group asked the reasonable question about whether they too would be trapped. Fortunately, Vos had done his research, gathered documents and cross-referenced them with the particular fervor of a man with nothing else to do, and the answer was firmly no. The curse was specific, it reached for those that were of House Corelanya. Then the question of why an Argonian from the Stone-Nest tribes had triggered it at all remained suspended in the air like smoke, too strange to press immediately. Once deliberation was settled he invited them all inside. The inside of the manor held its breath, dust moved like it had somewhere to be, ghosts appeared at the edges of vision with the disinterest of things that had been haunting for so long it had become habit rather than purpose. The halls held the specific weight of a place where knowledge had once been accumulated and then stopped accumulating abruptly. Vos had gathered what he could. He spread his documents across a table and explained what he had found: Valendir, the one who had placed the curse, was capable of granting exceptions. He had done it before. He was, presumably, still here in some form. The obstacle was location, Vos had searched the manor thoroughly and found no sign of him. The one place he hadn't checked was the gardens.

Once outside a plan started to form around the map Vos produced. Meanwhile Volg and Liliana found the edges of the conversation and used them, the way two people who share a particular nature sometimes do. They discussed what they were, what they owed each other by virtue, and what would happen if either of them forgot themselves. They reached the same understanding they had reached before, now more formally. It was a practical conversation between two people who respected practicality. Behind the group, Mia and Iris slowed. The distance between them had been accumulating for long enough that walking into a haunted manor was apparently the occasion that felt right to address a lingering issue. Mia opened something she had been keeping closed, not words exactly but the shape of what words would have to describe and let Iris feel the edges of it. The tragedy that a person carries changes how they look from the inside, her entire village being slaughtered, the death of her child. Understanding that requires proximity to that interior. Almost tearing up Iris gained a newfound understanding of what some of the things Mia had been through. While all this happened everyone followed Vos into the gardens, moving together toward something that had been waiting for long enough.
