The materials arrived at Pariah's Pinnacle, carried by Bayard, Gulraam, Lauren, Liliana, Mia, Vivienne, Volg, Wylarin, and Val the Hammer, the others having had their own business to attend to. The delivery itself was straightforward; the aftermath was less so. Mia found a specific corner of the clan's space for the baby echatere, somewhere it could be watched over without being underfoot, and settled it there. The echatere accepted this with the patience of an animal that has decided to wait. Liliana moved toward the kitchen immediately and found the state of it in her absence; she had opinions she was going to need to address immediately. The group dispersed into the space in the natural way of people who have been here before and know where they fit. Val looked through the giant door, and what she saw on the other side was the Hermaeus Mora statue at the center of the clan's worship space, and what it produced in her was immediate anger. She turned around and walked back toward the exit with the specific posture of someone who has already made a decision and is not interested in discussing it. Bayard tried to talk to her while Morning came over. Both of them said in differing words that these were people trying to survive as themselves, that no harm was intended, that the worship was theirs to have. Val listened to all of it and then walked out the door and closed it behind her with the kind of emphasis that is technically not a slam but functions as one. Gulraam, still operating at the consistent altitude he had maintained since Orsinium, followed her outside without appearing to fully understand why. Morning watched the door for a moment, said nothing, and walked to her tent. The group stood in the space she left and let the quiet settle.
Evening took them further in, and the people dispersed the way they do when a space has been established as somewhere they belong. Volg, Mia, Lauren, and Wylarin near the fire, falling into conversation with clan members who had been there long enough to stop being strangers. Liliana conducted her assessment of the kitchen with the focused displeasure of someone who has standards and has been away from them. Vivienne was located in the middle of the Orc children, who had resumed their wooden sword campaign against Bayard's patience and had apparently decided Vivienne was equally valid territory. The small Orc girl who had adopted her as a study subject at the previous visit was back at her post, copying every movement with the dedication of someone who had decided this was her area of specialization. The evening was settling into something resembling ordinary when a red mist came through the door. It arrived without knocking and moved with enough deliberateness that calling it weather was not an available option. It assembled itself into the shape of a person in the middle of the room with the quality of someone who had practiced this entrance. Evening's bow was up before the shape finished forming, the demand for identification delivered in the tone of someone who has had enough unusual things walk through his door recently and is not interested in adding to the list without explanation. The explanation, when it came, was this: the figure's name was Johann Wolvesbane, and he was looking for the same thing they had apparently encountered. The commotion pulled everyone in from wherever they had settled. Johann explained himself to the assembled group with the calm of someone accustomed to making his entrance create more alarm than he intended. He had been tracking the white figure, the large man, the wolf, whichever form it was currently wearing across Skyrim and through parts of Orsinium, following a trail of property damage and injuries that had been consistent throughout. However, interestingly enough, there were no deaths. Whatever this thing was, it had not killed anyone, which made it unusual for someone of his stature. He had followed the trail until it brought him to this group, which was the most recent point of contact he could identify. He was not certain what it was, but he knew the Fighters Guild had been logging the incidents separately and that those reports would contain more than he had assembled himself. Liliana fed everyone while they talked, which helped. Hot food has a specific effect on groups trying to make decisions; it does not resolve the decisions, but it makes the people making them calmer. The group ate and deliberated, and they agreed that the Fighters Guild was the obvious next step. The question was which one, Wrothgar, had the most relevant geography, but most Wrothgar Orcs had specific feelings about Clan Agalor's presence, and the existence of this conversation might create complications where none were needed to exist. Solitude was further but cleaner, a major Guild hall, a city large enough that the group could move through it without becoming a topic of conversation. Evening agreed with the direction and disagreed with one element of it. He asked that Clan Agalor not be mentioned in whatever inquiry they made. He said this in the way he says things when he has thought about them and is certain of their validity. Volg and Mia offered to stay with Evening and find a bar instead, which was presented as supporting his comfort.
Bayard decided he was going to find Val and have the conversation that the door-closing had interrupted. The rest of them collected Gulraam from just outside the entrance. He had not gone far and headed into the mountains toward Western Skyrim with Evening and Wylarin leading the way. Solitude received them with the particular energy of a city that has been important for a long time and knows it. Most of the group registered this as uncomfortable, which is the natural response to a place that is simultaneously too large and too watched. Volg mentioned, with a casualness that suggested he had been considering how to mention it, that he may or may not be wanted here. Evening told everyone to keep a low profile and meet back at the bar when they were done. Gulraam heard the word bar and walked directly into the nearest one at a volume that prompted several guards to immediately begin monitoring the situation. This was not keeping a low profile in any technical sense. It was, however, thoroughly consistent. The split happened outside: Volg, Mia, Evening, and Gulraam went to the bar. Johann, Lauren, Liliana, Vivienne, and Wylarin to the Fighters Guild hall. Inside the Guild hall, the approach was split further by natural temperament. Some people ask questions through the front door. Lauren made herself useful as a distraction, the specific kind of useful that involves occupying the attention of everyone in a room at once, which gave Wylarin room to operate in the peripheral spaces of the building with the unhurried efficiency she applies to most tasks that require not being noticed. What Wylarin found was a piece of paper referencing new locations being added to a compiled report on the creature, without specifying what those locations were. It confirmed the report existed and was ongoing, but it did not give them what they needed. What she also found nearby was a key to the hall. She noted this as potentially useful and put it where things that are potentially useful go, her pocket. Johann found Olred through the more direct approach of asking for whoever was responsible for the unusual incident reports. Olred's first response to the question about the white wolf figure was to lower his voice and look at the people in the nearest radius, which is what people do when they have been trying to keep something from becoming a general conversation and are not yet ready to give up on that ambition. He was not pleased that the question was being asked openly. What he said, once satisfied that the immediate radius was clear, was this: the incidents were real, the frequency was increasing, and keeping them separate in the public record was becoming harder. He had been in contact with Fighters Guild representatives in Wrothgar and had been hearing things at the edges of his correspondence from Vateshrans in the Reach, not formal accounts, but fragments of an oral tradition that connected to what he was seeing. His working theory was that what they were dealing with traced back to an old Reach story about a spirit of vengeance. He could say that the spirit could be summoned by anyone who knew the ritual and that the accounts suggested something larger operating behind it, a higher power of some kind, though the context was unclear and he was being careful about stating more than he could support. He offered to compile the locations where the creature had been confirmed and get them to the group; he just needed time to do it properly. In the next room, while this conversation was happening, Lauren had found something worth taking. One page from a document, not the full document, but something with a location written on it clearly enough to read. A site in Western Skyrim, just outside Ysmgar's Beach, she took it with her. The two halves of the group reconvened at the bar where Gulraam had made himself entirely at home, and Evening had found a quiet corner and stayed in it. They compared what each half had learned over the drinks Gulraam had been buying in the interval, at a volume that required some management, and put the pieces together. A location, a trail, and a name that Olred had not said directly but had pointed toward, something out of Reach oral tradition, something that resisted its own purpose, something being aimed by a hand that had not yet made itself fully visible. Solitude continued outside, unconcerned, while inside the bar, the group sat with what they had and decided what to do with it. Waiting for a report has a specific quality to it when the alternative is thinking too carefully about what the report might contain. The group managed this in various ways, with Mia waiting with the patient's stillness of someone who has decided that worrying is less useful than being ready. Gulraam managed it at the bar in the way he managed most things, which is to say without apparent concern for limits. Evening and Liliana managed it by discussing sweetrolls. The sweetrolls in question had been provided by the tavern with the confidence of an establishment that did not feel the need to check its own product before serving it. They were, upon investigation, approximately as sweet as stone and just as yielding. Liliana's feelings about this were detailed, but Evening tried, briefly, to give the kitchen the benefit of the doubt, which lasted until he attempted to eat one and discovered the benefit of the doubt was not structurally supported by the evidence. This was the state of things when Qrowe and Mevanos arrived. Liliana had sent word to both of them, Qrowe, because she knew his interest in things that might be undead, Mevanos, because his existing connection to Clan Agalor and his general appetite for unusual problems made him useful in the best sense. They came and found Liliana mid-rant about baked goods, which Mevanos received with the expression of someone who had information he was about to deploy at the wrong moment, which had been the cookies. There had apparently been an incident, some time ago, involving a batch of Liliana's own making that had been left long enough to develop strong opinions. The reminder produced a sweetroll travelling at considerable speed, which connected with Mevanos's head. Evening received this entire exchange with the genuine surprise of someone who has only ever known Liliana to produce food of considerable excellence and was not aware this spectrum extended in both directions. Mia, from across the table, observed that they were being remarkably casual given that something very large was currently somewhere in Skyrim and had recently had a greatsword positioned over Evening's chest. This was true, but the exchange continued anyway due to Evening's perceived safety from being in a city as well as being with his allies. Qrowe was reluctant about his name when Evening asked, but he eventually provided it. Liliana explained that she had brought both of them because they were mages and the investigation could use more of those. The group shared what they had assembled so far and decided the report was probably finished by now. Gulraam was invited to come along, but he declined by not getting up, and so they left him to it. Olred had finished when they arrived as he handed the papers to Evening with the manner of someone completing a transaction they are glad to have completed, he told them to be careful, that the figure might revisit these locations, that there was no official bounty yet because none of this had been released to the public, and he intended to keep it that way as long as that remained possible. Then he let them go to the first location, which took them to the coast at Ysmgar's Beach, which required first dealing with the troll Ysmgar, whose relationship with his own beach was apparently territorial enough to require resolution before any investigation could proceed. The resolution was achieved with his death, and the investigation followed. Along the shoreline, a series of ash rings marked the surrounding landscape in a pattern that was clearly made rather than found. The area itself was not hot as it should have been if fire had made those marks, leaving cold fire to be their own origin. The abilities they had witnessed on the Wrothgar road included cold fire, as the marks matched. The beach was a place he had stayed and practiced, and the evidence of it was burned into the ground without any warmth to show for it. Qrowe was looking at the ash rings when something at the edge of his attention redirected it. Chronus was approaching from the treeline with the careful posture of someone who was not certain of his welcome. He explained himself before anyone could ask; he had tracked them through Evening's Apocryphal energy, which was new enough and unstable enough that it left a trail he could follow. He wanted to help with the investigation, and Evening was more than willing to let him join. Evening found the rock, but he did not find what was written on it; the carving was there, clearly intentional, but the language was not one he could read. Qrowe felt something attached to it, a faint magical quality that confirmed the carving was not simply decorative, but he could not name what it was. Mevanos could read ancient Nordic, partially, which turned out to be exactly partially enough. He worked out what the rock said and told them. Solveig. The group catalogued it alongside everything else they had gathered and assigned it the status of something that was clearly significant to whoever carved it, without yet being significant to them, and so they wrote it down and moved on. The Reach location was a ruined tower off the main road between Skyrim and Markarth, not specified further in the report, but Qrowe spotted it first. As they approached, the signs of recent use became apparent; Liliana noticed that the campfire had been used within the last day, and Olred's warning about revisitation sat freshly in everyone's mind as they went inside. The group split naturally once inside, with Qrowe, Mevanos, and Liliana taking the lower chambers while Evening, Chronus, and Mia worked upstairs. Liliana lasted approximately as long as the dust required before the sneezing made her presence in the lower chamber a liability, and she stepped outside, which left Mevanos and Qrowe to find what was there. Mevanos found it on the wall. A drawing, not decorative but not the kind of thing someone makes without purpose. He reached for it using the auramancy that lets him access the intent of what was written, as the drawing opened to him. He saw three scenes all connected by lightning, the first was a hunting party somewhere in heavy forest, a thunderstorm overhead and the party already in trouble, beaten, injured, not where they intended to be. Lightning struck in front of them and set the forest burning, as the scene changed. The second was a ship in a storm, the crew was working against something larger than they were prepared for, the kind of fight between a vessel and weather that does not tend to go well for the vessel. Lightning hit the mast as the fire followed the fall, and suddenly the scene changed. The third was a room that was not a room, dark, cold, wet, with nothing in it that could be identified with a silence that had weight. Until something in the darkness moved, and the sound of it produced a growl. The eyes that appeared were the specific green of something that had been watching for longer than time itself. The creature came forward out of the darkness as it came toward him and lunged for his throat.
Just before it arrived, the vision ended, and Mevanos stood where he had been standing and relayed what he had seen to Qrowe. Upstairs, Chronus had been working through a pile of papers and books with the methodical patience of someone who knew his way around books. He eventually found something at the bottom of the pile, a stone, flat, with the four cardinal directions marked on it in the specific arrangement of a navigational tool. Not decorative, not symbolic in the way objects made for rituals are symbolic, it was functional. Due to his Maormer heritage, he recognized this as either a Lodestone, or something even older, a Waystone. He brought it downstairs, and they assembled what they had. The report's confirmation of a summoned creature of vengeance using cold fire, ice, and fog. The restraint, no deaths, active holding back, a will operating against its own direction. The name Solveig was carved more carefully than anything else he left behind. The Lodestone, belonging to someone who travelled by sea, was carried across for however long it had been without losing its function. The three scenes, a hunting party in a forest whose location Mevanos believed he could identify, a ship going down in a storm, and a wolf creature with green eyes coming out of the dark, the moment before the vision ended. They stood in the lower chamber of a ruined tower in the Reach with a Lodestone and a list and three images burned into Mevanos's memory by a drawing on a wall, and the picture they were assembling had a shape now, even if the shape did not yet have a name.
