The days at Pariah's Pinnacle had settled into the particular rhythm of people who had decided, collectively, that a job needs doing and they were the ones to do it. The cold was still there, the cramped quarters were still there, the cave-in was still a cave-in, but within all of that, something had shifted in the specific way things shift when enough hands are applied, and enough food is being made. Bayard, Lauren, Ra'khadar, and Volg moved stone alongside Yashna and the clan members who had decided to join in, which was more of them every day. There is a quality to heavy work done alongside people you are still learning, it bypasses a great deal of the usual social machinery and gets directly to the part where you know whether someone is worth knowing. Chronus, Mevanos, and Lorzub had turned their attention to the problem of Hermaeus Mora's attention, specifically how to get it back, which required an understanding of what had been lost and what might replace it. Mevanos had contributed several of his own texts on the subject of Arcanist abilities operating independently of Ruins, which Lorzub had received with wide eyes; she had not put them down since. Evening and Wylarin took the mountain; they had been patrolling the perimeter with the quiet efficiency of two people who understand that protection is mostly noticing things before they become problems. What they noticed, over several days, was that the same insignia kept appearing on multiple separate scouts at multiple separate positions around the mountain. They interrogated a few, and their answers pointed to a mercenary company called the Grey Snow. They seemed to be interested in the area for reasons that nobody they questioned seemed willing or able to fully specify. The clan was not in a position to pursue it further, so they simply noted it and kept watching. Through the portal came more. Vivienne and Yavna arrived and found their footing with a clan that would take help wherever they could find it. The clan, for its part, absorbed them without ceremony, through proximity and shared work, no matter the size. The Orc children had opinions about the new arrivals, where a group of them had already decided that Bayard was the appropriate target for wooden sword combat, a conclusion they had reached when they saw his shiny knight armor. He received this with what could generously be called grace and entertained them and even seemed to enjoy himself as he did. Vivienne acquired her own shadow in the form of a small Orc girl who had taken one look at her once and made a decision: she wanted to be a vampire when she grew up. The girl copied her movements with the focused attention of a dedicated student, which is either the most flattering thing that can happen to a person or the most unsettling, depending entirely on the movements being copied. Liliana's food and Morning's drinks did the rest; there is a particular alchemy in good food made with attention in a space that has been short on comfort. As a result, what it produced in the main room of Pariah's Pinnacle was the kind of merriment that doesn't announce itself but fills a room regardless. The clan laughed and finally enjoyed themselves, and for a few days, the refugee camp felt, briefly, genuinely, like something else. Then the wind came through, it was magical in the way that is immediately apparent even to people who cannot name what they are sensing, a quality to it beyond temperature and direction, something that carried intent the way ordinary wind does not. It moved through the entirety of Pariah's Pinnacle with the unhurried thoroughness of something that knew exactly where it was going and wanted to make sure it reached everyone.
Then it settled, and in the space it left behind came a voice, not heard exactly, more arrived, directly, warmly, slightly awkwardly phrased in the specific way Vos-Greel is slightly awkwardly phrased when he is trying to say something that matters to him. The Topee-Rajta Baths were open, and everyone was welcome. The discussion that followed was brief; they had earned it. From Clan Agalor: Lorzub, Murgakh, Shel, Shuvra, Morning, and Evening wanted to go as well; again, all were invited. The rest stayed to keep the work moving. Yashna did not comment on this but decided to stay behind, which showed her dedication to this project. Through Wrothgar and down to the coast and then by boat across to Solstice, where Vos was waiting outside with his arms open and the expression of someone for whom this moment had been the point of a great deal of preparation. The Topee-Rajta Baths announced themselves in the blend of their architecture before anything else. Corelanya structure in the bones of it, high proportions, precise stonework, the particular aesthetic confidence of a clan that had once considered itself exceptional, layered over with the warmth of Stone-Nest Argonian design, living elements encouraged into the walls and ceilings, water integrated rather than contained. Two traditions that had never been intended to share a building did so with unexpected success. Vos walked them through it with the pride of someone showing something they built, which, in the sense that matters, he had. Outside first, heated pools fed from deep within the island, the water carrying a faint luminescence from the Bismuth formations growing naturally along the edges. The rejuvenating quality was real; even standing near it felt like something the shoulders had been waiting for. Beside the outdoor pool, a small cart stood attended by a Sloth of apparent intelligence and total composure, managing an inventory of potions, lotions, and drinks appropriate to the heat with the calm efficiency of someone who had found their calling and was at peace with it despite being a simple Sloth. Inside: a changing room, a kitchen stocked generously and specifically, a steam room temperature-controlled by the island's own waters. A shallow pool for sitting and conversation and a main pool, deep enough for swimming, anchored by a four-headed stone statue that poured water from all four mouths, which brought an odd sort of peace to the room. Vos gave them the rules with the gentle firmness of a man who has thought carefully about what specifically needs to be said. No running, no indecency, no drinking all of the alcohol, no harassment of staff or guests, and no entering the employee areas. Then, with that, he let them go, but not all of them. Shel, Shuvra, Morning, and Evening, he took them aside for a private conversation in a different part of the building. What was said there was said without the rest of them present. The groups dispersed the way groups do when given warm water and no agenda. Vivienne, Bayard, and Ra'khadar claimed the main pool. Volg, Mia, and Lauren went to the steam baths. Wylarin and Liliana found the shallow pool. Meanwhile, Chronus, Mevanos, and Yavna took the outside. In the shallow pool, Liliana encountered water in the way that people who are afraid of water encounter it, with the specific tension that froze your entire body. What arrived beside her was Erevyn Thalas, a kind older Altmer woman who noticed the tension with the attention of someone who has spent a long time learning how to read what people are not saying. She did not address the fear directly; she simply started talking about something else entirely, the way experienced people redirect attention, not away from the thing but toward something more interesting. The water, meanwhile, became less important. Erevyn described her work with the ease of someone who has explained it many times and finds it genuinely worth explaining. She was the public face and manager of a map-making company, she said, The Wandering Compass. Found all across Tamriel, it is a very old institution, where most adventurers found their maps. She said this last part with the slight smile of someone who has a more complete version of the sentence available to them and is choosing not to deliver it. Nearby, Wylarin bumped into a younger Bosmer woman in a particular way that was completely accidental. Her name was Web, and she was a dancer and entertainer from Vvardenfell. They talked for a long while about each other, unhurried, interested, and covering more ground than the time should have allowed. After a long enough time, Chronus, Yavna, Mevanos, and Lorzub drifted to the outdoor pool, talking amongst themselves. A Dunmer man had been watching their conversation with the patience of someone deciding whether the approach was worth making; he eventually decided it was. He called them over and introduced himself as Drevna, a senior employee at The Wandering Compass, handling finances among other things. He had been working on some maps and wanted details from well-travelled people. Mevanos, Yavna, and Chronus helped him. Drevna offered to put their names in the footnotes, but they declined, which he received as graciously as the offer had been made. The conversation ended well, and he returned to wherever he had been. In the steam baths, the heat did what heat does to the space between people, making the distance feel smaller. Volg, Mia, and Lauren had been talking when Murgakh arrived in nothing but a bathrobe and the particular confidence of a man who has decided he has earned exactly this. He settled in with them and said what older people sometimes say when they have been given something genuinely good, that he was enjoying himself, that this was a fine place, that Vos had done well with it. Then he paused in the specific way he pauses, the one that means he is about to say the thing underneath the thing, and mentioned that there was something about Vos he could not quite place. He did not elaborate further; he did not need to. Most of the others knew what he was talking about and seemed to agree with him. There was something about Vos that he was not revealing to everyone. He settled back into the steam and let the heat do its work. Vos's voice came through the facility with the particular quality of an announcement that has been rehearsed enough to sound natural. Everyone was invited outside for a special performance by Web from Vvardenfell. They all gathered at the large open Stone-Nest stage with food and drinks arranged along the edges, with the attention of someone who knew how to make a space feel like an occasion. Web and the band assembled, and the performance began. As the group watched her, something happened that was difficult to describe. It was simply that Web had a face that several people in the Sugar Falls group found familiar in the specific way that means their brain was trying to locate a memory and finding the edges of it blurry. The more directly anyone tried to remember where they had seen her, the less clearly they could hold the image. The face was familiar; that much was obvious, but the memory of why it was familiar was not there. The performance itself caused the entire room to be encapsulated toward her; the food and drinks suddenly became secondary, and the space felt full of something that had not been there before she started. She came down into the crowd eventually and tried to dance with a few of the people in the crowd. Regardless of whether they decided to dance with her or not, nobody left the space feeling anything other than that this had been exactly the right thing to be doing this afternoon. Then, in the distance, someone noticed about five ships coming toward the island. The wrong kind of flags for a trading vessel, the wrong kind of formation for anything routine. As they came closer, the details became readable to anyone who had seen that kind of imagery recently enough to recognize it, Worm Cult. Whatever the day had been, it was not finished yet.
The music quickly stopped; however, the ships did not. Three groups formed with the quick and slightly imperfect logic of people who have to make decisions faster than they would like. Two ships breaking east took Lauren, Liliana, Ra'khadar, Volg, and Yavna with them. One ship heading west pulled Morning, Evening, Vos, and the Clan Agalor members in that direction. That left two ships coming straight in, and Bayard, Mevanos, Mia, Vivienne, Wylarin, and Web on the steps of the Topee-Rajta Baths to receive them. Mia moved first with a dagger assembled from her blood with the particular efficiency of someone who has done this enough times, and threw it with the typical force of a vampire. The aftermath being one of the ships had a mast that was being held up by the collective effort of several very stressed Worm Cultists working against the decision gravity was trying to make. Bayard, recognizing that he could not properly help without his armor, ran inside to collect his. Vivienne simply became what she had always hidden, a Vampire Lord, The Pale Lady, and the Worm Cultists on the nearest ship received the experience of looking at something that quickly changed the quality of their afternoon. Mevanos reached into the primordial chaos and pulled something through, an Ash Titan, though the specific Ash Titan he got was smaller than expected and had the energy of someone who had been desperately hoping for a different assignment. A look from Mevanos moved it forward, but as it inched closer, a ballista bolt from the ship dissipated it immediately. Mevanos noted this with the expression of a man adding something to a mental list of grievances. Wylarin found elevation on the nearby Stone-Nest structure and used it to gain elevation over the entire beach. From up there, she could see the whole picture, and in the middle of that picture, standing on the deck of the lead ship with the specific posture of someone who has been planning this moment and is disappointed by how it is going, was a familiar face. The Worm Cult Leader from Corelanya Manor. The one they had left dangling from a catwalk in burning robes, he was back, which suggested that humiliation had only sharpened whatever motivated him. Wylarin took a shot at him, and immediately, he noticed her. She gave him a wave, and that single wave made sure that if he had not been having a good day before, he was now having a much worse one. The water became a problem for the Worm Cultists before they could make it a problem for anyone else. Mia's lightning went into it at the same moment Mevanos found the geothermal architecture of the island and adjusted it. The water between the ships and the shore became somewhere a person with good judgment would not wade into, and led to multiple cultists' deaths. It limited their options considerably, which was the point, though it left the water full of things that would normally represent an opportunity for Mia's particular skill set. However, in this case, it did not; Necromancy, as it turned out, found no purchase in the Topee-Rajta Baths. Whatever she reached for was simply not there, but the interesting symmetry of this was that it applied to the Worm Cultists equally; their own necromantic toolkit would not work here either. They were limited to what they had, which became clear that it was increasingly not enough. Their leader, Bezlin, though the group did not have a name for him yet, made a decision. He levitated the remaining forces over the water and laid a ward over them on the shore, which was both a blessing and a curse as they tried to survive the onslaught from the group.
Web, watching this from the edge, made her own decision and moved toward the ship while everyone else was looking at the ward. She was aboard before anyone could notice and started sabotaging and dispatching with the graceful efficiency of an experienced dancer. Bayard soon came back outside. The difference between Bayard without armor and weapons and Bayard with armor and weapons is the kind of difference that seemed to inspire hope in his fellow comrades. A small group of Worm Cultists made it out from behind the ward and toward the main house. They soon met an Aedric Spear on its way through, and a wide sweep of his sword made sure that they did not continue past that point with their heads attached to their bodies. Mia turned her attention to the ones still standing behind the ward and found that vampiric charm worked perfectly well where the necromancy did not, which narrowed the options of several Worm Cultists in a way they had not anticipated. Wylarin kept taking shots at Bezlin from above with the patient consistency of someone who has found something worth doing and is doing it thoroughly. Seeing this, Bezlin gave an order, and the trebuchets fired. Flaming rocks came down across the island with the indiscriminate enthusiasm of a strategy applied by someone who has stopped caring about collateral damage. Several things on the small island caught fire: small buildings on the beach, the surrounding wildlife, Mevanos's cloak, most importantly. He looked down at it, he looked at the ships, and he added this to his growing mental list. Meanwhile, Vivienne had gathered a meaningful quantity of bats from the island, organized, purposeful, and completely under her control. While Mevanos had hands made of sand working their way under the ward from below. Bezlin stood in the middle of this situation that had started as revenge and was ending as an object lesson in proportionality, and he made the only decision left to him, and he gave the retreat. The ships turned and gathered together as they were moving away. However, Vivienne wouldn’t let them. Vivienne sent the bats. What followed was not something the Worm Cultists on those ships had prepared for. The bats moved with the velocity of something that had a great deal of momentum and a specific destination, and when they arrived at their destinations, they went through rather than stopping. Two ships took damage, and very quickly they went down. Two more made it away; however, one remained, listing but afloat, in a state that could generously be described as salvageable. On the abandoned ship, Web stepped back onto the shore having done what she had gone to do. She watched the last of the bats work and had the expression of someone for whom this was professionally interesting if not slightly disturbing. The groups came back together on the steps of the Topee-Rajta Baths. Everyone who had gone east came back, and everyone who had gone west came back. Vos came out to meet them, where he thanked them with the genuine warmth of a man who means it and is also very aware of what his property has just been through. He apologized for the turn the evening had taken, which was also genuine. Then he offered them the salvageable Worm Cult ship, which he framed as a practical solution; he wanted no evidence of the attack remaining near his bathhouse. Wylarin had noticed that Vos was out of breath, not from running; there was no indication he had been anywhere that required running and not from fear, his composure was intact. The out-of-breath quality was the specific kind that comes after something difficult, the way a person breathes after they have been doing something that costs rather than something that moves. It passed quickly, and he was already talking about the ship. The group noted it without pressing it, because the evening had given them enough to process. The conversation then turned to what Vos had discussed in private with Shel, Morning, and Evening during the bathhouse tour, which resolved into specifics: materials for the rebuilding effort at Pariah's Pinnacle, scrolls from House Corelanya, and information from the Sea and Sword Lodge in Solstice. He offered it as though it were simply the natural conclusion of being a person who had things and knew people who needed them. Morning and Evening received it with the gratitude of people who have been carrying things for long enough that being offered help with it produces a response more complex than simple thanks. The group loaded everything onto the Worm Cult boat and went through it while they worked. Stripped of its insignia so that it would be a reasonable travel vessel.
However, it was while going through it that the group found the letter, which was written in the specific tone of a man who has been building toward something and has finally decided it is time. Bezlin, with this letter, they now had a name for the face that had been glaring at them from the catwalk in Corelanya Manor. He had written to his forces explaining what they were doing and why. The revenge element was present, as expected, but what had not been expected was the other reason. A significant quantity of Worm Cult resources had been stolen, and the trail of those resources had reportedly led here, to the Topee-Rajta Baths. The group stood with this for a moment; they looked at the bathhouse, they looked at each other. Vos was inside, talking warmly with whoever was still near him, offering drinks, being entirely himself. The question of why Worm Cult resources would be stored underneath a Stone-Nest Argonian bathhouse run by a man who seemed unassociated with that kind of life simply added to the questions that surrounded this person. They loaded the rest of the supplies onto the boat and set course for Wrothgar, carrying the question with them across the water.
