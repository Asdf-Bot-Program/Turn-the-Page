The gardens of Corelanya Manor had the particular quality of a place that had been left to its own devices for long enough that it had developed opinions. The hedges grew with the confidence of things that answered to no one, and whatever had once been an orderly arrangement of paths had long since become a suggestion rather than a fact. The group moved through it the way people move through somewhere they have been told is dangerous, carefully. The skeletons and ghosts that called the gardens home were not dramatic about it. They proceeded in the functional way that combat sometimes does when the people involved know what they are doing. However, this time the walls reached out, three hands, bony and sudden, extended from the dense green and closed around Bayard, Iris, and Rali with the unhurried certainty of something that had been waiting for exactly this, then they were simply elsewhere. Not gone, Mia confirmed that much, reaching across using her vampiric sense, she sensed out and found them present and intact in another part of the manor. Volg issued the warning to the rest, with the economy of someone more interested in solving the problem than describing it, as the group pressed on.

The dead end arrived as dead ends do, without apology, and what it contained was, ironically enough, a rat. The rat sat with the exhausted patience of someone who has been watching a situation deteriorate for longer than is reasonable and has finally decided to say something about it. It opened its mouth and told them, in a voice that carried the specific weariness of custodial disappointment, to stop making a mess of its gardens. They questioned the rat, as one does and as they do, a small orb separated itself from the creature and assembled, with the unhurried dignity of someone who has been a ghost long enough to know there is no particular rush, into the form of Valendir. The owner of the manor and the author of the curse. A man who had, apparently, been watching them as a rat, and had opinions about what he had seen.

They explained the situation, Vos-Greel, stuck, not by choice, in need of an exception to the terms Valendir himself had written. Valendir brought Vos forward and examined him with the attention of a man reading an important document. The curse was on him; that much was obvious, but the blood was not Corelanya. He stood with this contradiction for a moment, the way a scholar stands with a calculation that refuses to resolve correctly. He was confused while also, despite himself, intrigued. He agreed to help, as people sometimes do when something doesn't make sense and the only way to understand it better is to stay involved. The price was a choice: the bushes in the garden, trimmed by hand, today, or the Worm Cultists that had apparently made themselves comfortable in the lower levels of the manor, in the crypt. The group looked at the state of the gardens. They chose the Worm Cultists.

Vos, it turned out, would not be joining them. The crypt required going outside, and outside remained outside his reach. He accepted this with the particular resignation of someone who has spent enough time trapped to have made peace with individual disappointments. Granny Lexy elected to stay with him, which she presented as companionship and which Valendir experienced as something else entirely. Once inside the crypt greeted them with the standard of somewhere that had been neglected in one direction and overcrowded in another. Summoned things moved through the dark with the purpose of creatures that had been told to be there. Then, further in, something unexpected: Worm Cultists on the ground, and the reason they were on the ground still very much in motion. Lauren moved through the space with the focused efficiency of someone who had come here for a specific reason and was not interested in anything that interfered with it. The kind of competence that doesn't announce itself, and when the last of them were dealt with, she turned, and the sword and shield turned with her, pointed at the group with the same efficiency she had just applied to everything else. What followed was the particular challenge of a conversation where one party has words, and the other has hands, and the two are not immediately compatible. Lauren was mute; she communicated in sign language with the calm of someone for whom this was simply how things worked. Mia, Wylarin, and Mevanos understood her. The rest understood Mia, who translated with the matter-of-fact steadiness of someone more interested in the content of what was being said than the novelty of how it was being said. What Lauren was saying was this: she was here for something that might help her reclaim a connection to magic she had lost years ago. The how of that loss was hers to keep for now. They explained their own reasons for being there, and when the explanations satisfied each other sufficiently, they continued together.

The leader of this particular pocket of Worm Cult had the energy of a man who had constructed a situation he was proud of and was not prepared to have it dismantled. The room he occupied held two things worth noting: a large tree climbing toward the ceiling with a strange blue crack of light snaking through it like a frozen lightning strike, and several papers resting on a nearby empty grave. He cast a spell before the fight properly began. The spell filled the room around him and set itself to work; whatever damage came in was neutralized, healed as if it never happened. As the fight went on, it was Wylarin and Lauren who found the answer, the way two people sometimes find an answer when they are both looking at the same problem from useful angles. A rope and a shield were the answer, the geometry of where the spell worked and where it stopped working. Wylarin got the rope around him; meanwhile, Lauren took the other end, attached it to her shield, and threw the shield with precision. It got caught in the nearby catwalk, and the rope went taut. The Worm Cult Leader went with it, dangling, no longer within the comfortable range of his own protection. The group proceeded, as agreed, to address this by beating the everliving shit out of him. Mevanos finished it with fire that was so bright it might as well have been day. The robes went first, and his dignity went with them. The Worm Cult Leader looked down at himself, assessed the situation, made a decision, and teleported away.

Alone in the room, the group turned their attention to the tree. The blue crack in its bark was not natural, which was apparent, and was not accidental, which took slightly longer to establish. The Worm Cultists had been using a weak point in the curse's architecture, something Valendir had either left incomplete. As well as a portal, pointed somewhere the group did not particularly want to go. Mevanos looked at it the way he looked at most things that could be taken apart and put back together differently. He worked at the spell until it pointed somewhere more useful, outside the manor and away from the curse. The group stood with this for a moment. They had done what Valendir had asked of them, but they also had this new option that would completely bypass him. They weighed both of their options. Valendir was an old necromancer from Clan Corelanya. There was a good possibility that he wouldn’t keep up his word. Plus, there was a matter of pride; why use his help when they could do it all on their own? However, on the other side, the ghost of an ancient necromancer was not someone that you would want to make upset. After much deliberation, the answer they arrived at was honesty to Valednir, with the alternate exit kept quietly in reserve, the way a sensible person keeps a second option, simply present.

Granny Lexy was in the main hall when they returned, comfortable in the way that suggested she had been comfortable for some time. Vos, however, was not. She had mentioned that she last saw him in the way of the dining hall, so the group started there. Once inside, they saw him emerging from the direction of the underground laboratory, the Reanimatorium, at a speed that suggested urgency, pivoted when he saw them, and adopted the posture of someone who had been looking at the nearby statues this whole time and found them very interesting. Volg looked at him with distrust and asked what was going on. Vos offered the explanation that he is part of the Fighters Guild and finding the Voskrona Eyes was simply part of his job. The group received this explanation with the polite skepticism it warranted. Valendir materialized with the specific energy of someone who had been watching the entire time and had been wondering when to mention it. He registered his disappointment about the weak spot and the thoughts that had been considered as an escape option, but he granted the exception anyway. He also made sure to mention the three that had been separated from them once they entered the gardens: Bayard, Iris, and Rali. They had apparently had their own adventure of mayhem throughout the manor, and Valendir had had enough of them and wanted them out as well. A secret passageway opened up, and out walked the missing three, looking a tad bit more traumatized than before. 

However, despite that, Vos-Greel was finally able to walk through the door after all this time. The relief that came off him was the kind that has been compressed for long enough that it arrives all at once. He thanked them with the sincerity of someone who means it more than they know how to say. Then came the matter of compensation, as promised, one free day at the bathhouse was met with a wall of silent consensus that it was insufficient. Three days was met the same kind of silence; the negotiation moved through several positions with the particular momentum of a group that had just spent time in a crypt and had opinions about what that was worth. The wall held until Vos arrived at an offer that the group found, at last, proportionate: a free trip, any time, forever, and with that, the wall finally came down. As the group made their way back, Mevanos stopped walking and turned around. Some people leave a place and take what the experience gave them. Mevanos was the kind of person who went back in; he descended again into the Reanimatorium with the focused purpose of someone who knows what they are looking for and intends to find it. The same area that Vos was seen coming out of. When he emerged, he carried a scroll, a document that concerned itself with the movement and control of souls in ways that most institutions would consider beyond their scope. Soul transfer, not only that, but also soul domination. He had a pleasant conversation with Valendir on the way out and parted on reasonable terms. He went back to his tower, scroll in hand, with the expression of a man who considered the day well spent.
