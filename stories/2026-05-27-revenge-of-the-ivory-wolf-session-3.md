The island was quiet in the way things go quiet after something violent has moved through and decided to stop. The group pulled themselves together on the steps of the Topee-Rajta Baths, took stock of who was where, and made plans. Resources needed to be moved, and carts needed to be loaded. The former Worm Cult ship, stripped of its insignia and repurposed, was waiting at the dock. Most of Clan Agalor headed back under their own arrangements while Morning and Evening stayed to help move the materials. The rest sorted themselves out with Lauren and Yavna staying on to accompany the caravan. The carts were loaded, the ship was boarded, and they set out for Wrothgar carrying Corelanya scrolls and building materials.
Orsinium received them with the stonework alone, stopping Bayard in the middle of the street, which is where a larger Orc male found him. The Orc introduced himself as Kushrakh gro-Morkul. He had been with the Grey Snow until they left him in an arena in Craglorn without looking back, and sought revenge for their transgression. Shel had reached him and asked him to meet the caravan and help see the resources safely to Clan Agalor. He was here. The group was still getting acquainted with Kushrakh when Bayard and Vivienne noticed two faces in the crowd. The kind of noticing that comes from recognition rather than strangeness, as they saw a fully armoured Nord named Val the Hammer and an Orc named Gulraam, who was carrying himself as someone who had been drinking for long enough that drinking became a full-time occupation. The group had explained their purpose here, as the two agreed to help them in their endeavors as the caravan moved out of Orsinium, carrying considerably more people than it had arrived with. They talked as they went, the way people talk while on roads, aimless but still pleasant. The path took them past Clan Shatul. Morning and Evening passed without comment, trying to hide from prying eyes, and then down toward a small bridge over a gap in the terrain. They were crossing the bridge when they all noticed a large figure standing on the ruins of a nearby building, a little further down the road, simply watching them. Nearly seven feet tall with long straight white hair and eyes that narrowed to a point. Armor that was old and worn in a way that suggests it has been old and worn for a very long time. It was all white, with gold accents that were worn with time. Beside him, a greatsword nearly as tall as he was, made entirely of ice, rested against the stone with the ease of something he carried the way other people carry a walking stick. Yavna and Mia called out to ask what he was doing, but he said nothing. He stepped off the building with the thud of his landing, traveling through the ground and up through the soles of their feet. The surrounding air began to change before anyone could process the landing. Cold arrived, not the Wrothgar cold, not the Skyrim cold; this was older and more specific. The kind of cold that has no season. It reached into the spaces between things and pulled the warmth out of them directly. He took out the greatsword, and suddenly, he was no longer a man as he took on the form of a wolf. The wolf was enormous and moved with the specific speed of something that has decided where it is going and intends to be there immediately. It rushed into the middle of the group, and ice came with it, spreading from its movement, reaching outward, catching several of them in the frozen ground before they could adjust. Then it walked, slow, deliberate circles. Eyes moving across each of them in turn, with the particular attention of someone reading a document rather than regarding an obstacle, then he walked into a nearby tree and was gone.
The group held its position. Whatever he was, he was still somewhere in these woods and had given every indication he would be back. Mia set a blood spell to punish anything that stepped into their space. Mevanos, Vivienne, Morning, and Evening pulled back to the carts. Gulraam, for his part, had processed the situation in his own way, which was to sit down and fall asleep, his considerable contributions to the defence of the caravan having apparently concluded for the evening. Bayard readied an Aedric Spear and waited, in response the wolf emerged. He came through directly toward Bayard, Bayard threw the spear, and immediately the ice greatsword came up and deflected it into the ground with the ease of someone who had been watching the shoulder and knew exactly when it was coming. He landed in front of Bayard and said, for the first time, in a voice that carried the flat quality of a statement rather than a taunt, "Aedric magics can't defend against everything." He then breathed cold fire from his mouth across the blade and swung. The cold fire reached further than the sword did, with several people caught in the edge of it. Then he simply stood there and waited for them to come to him, watching. Mevanos, at a distance, watched the exchange with the specific attention of someone assembling a picture from available evidence. The picture that assembled itself was that this was not a life-or-death fight, at least for him. This was something else, a test of sorts, he guessed. He considered this, summoned a shield to protect the carts, and returned to his book.
Val came in first with the hammer, a downward strike with enough weight behind it to leave no doubt of the impact a hit like that would have. However, ice answered before the hammer could; it rose from the ground and formed a wall. The strike broke against it, leaving him unmoved. Mia took a different approach as a blood blade assembled itself, and she blinked through the space between them, arriving inside his guard. He moved at the same time with his arm, found hers in the crook of it, twisted, and she was pushed back before the blade had anywhere to go. He looked at her and said, "Keep using your blood like that, and it won't be there when you really need it." The correction was delivered in the same voice as everything else, not unkind, simply accurate. Wylarin had decided to keep her distance; she backflipped and released an arrow at the peak of it. His head moved first, a small adjustment that took him off the arrow's path entirely. He looked at her and said, "Distance allows for recovery. Close the distance." Lauren had been watching with the quality of someone who processes a fight differently than most people, less instinct, more architecture. She threw her shield, and he moved out of its path with the ease of someone who had seen this exact attack in a dozen different forms. What he had not accounted for was that moving out of its path was the point; the shield ricocheted off the stone behind him, and the strap attached to it came back with it, and when it arrived, it wrapped around him and held. He looked at her and gave her a nod. In a fight where he had corrected almost everything, the nod landed differently than any words had. Yavna took the opportunity of him being held in place as she went invisible and came in from behind. He noticed at the last moment with an unnatural sense and brought the greatsword behind him. The blade blocked the strike as the ice chipped. He looked back at her and said, "Backstabbing will get you nowhere." Kushrakh had been watching all of this with the level head of someone who has been in enough bad situations to know when theatrical engagement is available as an alternative to panic. He decided to try to draw the focus to himself, which, in response, the figure looked at him with the first suggestion of something other than assessment, something closer to interest, and entertained the challenge. He disappeared into the ground, dropping Lauren’s shield to the ground and reforming out of the face of a large rock beside Kushrakh, coming out of the stone itself the way he had come out of the tree while in his wolf form. He let out an ungodly howl in what seemed to be an attempt at showmanship but sounded like something straight out of a nightmare. But Kushrakh stayed strong as he lunged towards him in his human form. Kushrakh blocked the attack and locked weapons with him, their faces were close enough for a quiet exchange with him saying, "The longer you wait, the more people will die." It was the quietest thing he had said and the one that carried the most weight. Kushrakh's stance faltered as the figure pushed him back and broke the lock. He looked at everyone with that same uninterested assessment, then he shifted back to wolf form and began to circle. Slowly at first, then with accumulating speed, the cold fire trail from his movement marked a ring around the group, then the salt water came. It arrived without announcement, without explanation, from nowhere that made any physical sense. Ocean water in the mountain road, covering the landscape, cold and dark, and carrying the specific smell of open sea that had no business being here at any elevation in Wrothgar. He reformed into his human shape in the middle of it and drove the greatsword into the water, causing everything to freeze. Several people broke free through a combination of strength and urgency, but everyone had ice in the joints of their armor, in the folds of their clothing, against their skin, a slowing weight that was not restraint but held fast against them. Almost as if out of nowhere, a small echatere emerged from between the trees, all white in color, young, moving with the confidence of a creature too small to have fully learned what caution is for. It found the figure's legs and leaned against them the way cats lean against people they have decided belong to them. He looked down at it and something changed in him briefly, in the small way that a person changes when something unexpected happens that requires care rather than strategy. He shooed it away gently, toward the tree line, not wanting it in the space of what was about to happen. The echatere went behind a tree and waited there, apparently satisfied. He turned toward the carts, specifically toward Morning and Evening. He moved at Morning first, but she was ready, an Apocryphal blast released before he fully committed, missing but catching him off the trajectory he had planned. He adjusted mid-movement and disappeared into the ground rather than landing where he had intended. He came back up out of a rock beside Evening and kicked. The kick connected before Evening had time to do anything with the warning and went down. The figure stood over him with his greatsword raised, and Evening looked up at him from the ground with the expression of someone who is not going to look away from what is coming; however, the blade did not come down. He stopped, the same stop that seemed to hold everything else in its grasp, not long, but real. He looked at the expression on Evening's face for a moment that was longer than anything else in the fight had been. Then he said the only thing he had said that was not a correction, not a demonstration, not a statement about technique or capability or consequence, "I'm sorry,” and he was gone before the words finished arriving. Morning pulled Evening up off the ground and held onto him for a moment in the uncomplicated way of someone who had just watched something she was not ready to watch and needed a second to confirm he was still there. The group gathered themselves around the question of what exactly had just happened; they all came up blank for explanations. The search of the ruined building produced a recently used campsite, fire remnants, and the signs of someone who had been sleeping there. Whatever he was, he was not a ghost; ghosts do not camp, that much is obvious. The echatere was still behind the tree; it had waited through all of it with perfect patience, as though it had nowhere else to be and had decided this was the right place to remain. Kushrakh produced snow truffles from somewhere on his person, and the echatere emerged from the tree and allowed itself to be collected. They loaded back up and continued toward Pariah's Pinnacle. The smell of salt water was still faintly on the cold air behind them, and the small white echatere rode in the cart among the Corelanya scrolls and building materials, as nobody said much for a while.
