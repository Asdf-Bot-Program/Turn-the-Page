backstory: >-
  Both twins were born in 2E 552 in the Shatul Clan to Yegoth and Shel Agalor,
  who were only together due to an arranged marriage. From a very young age, the
  two were taught by their Father to be warriors for the Shatul Clan. Yegoth had
  a plan for his children that was to be followed, so they could become the
  warriors he had always intended to raise. However, Shel didn’t follow that
  plan; she was always seen as different, wanting to live outside of the rigid
  structure that Orc society had set up for them, always wanting to learn new
  things and not focusing on combat, but being too timid to do anything about
  it. It was very clear from the beginning that the two were not going to agree
  with each other. Even more so when Morngdub and Evnigdub were born, each
  parent saw a different way to raise their children. 


  Morngdub always wanted to stick with her mother; she idolized her and everything that she did. Shel started teaching her different things from her personal collections, and Morngdub gained a fascination with knowledge and learning, something that wasn’t looked upon kindly in Orc society. However, unlike her mother, who was timid in nature, Morngdub had a personality similar to her father, headstrong and determined to see things her way no matter what. Morngdub and her father would oftentimes clash heads with each other, and even though Morngdub was still a child, she would hold her own in verbal arguments, determined to see the argument through even if it took hours on end.


  Evnigdub, on the other hand, was much more like his Mother personality-wise, very quiet and timid, always wanting to please the people around him at the cost of his own well-being. Being male, his Father took the most interest in him regardless of how Evnigdub felt about it. His Father taught him how to be a warrior just like he had planned, and while Evnigdub passed every single one of his Father’s tests, it wasn’t something that interested him. As time went on, Evnigdub adopted a mindset of efficiency, completing whatever task his Father gave him as efficiently as possible. This sort of compliance with what was expected of him overwrote any opinion that he might have had. He had opinions about certain things, but he was taught that none of it mattered; the only thing that did was the betterment of the Clan.


  One day, all the problems that this family had came to a breaking point. Yegoth had found out that both Shel and Morngdub had denounced their worship of both Malacath and Trinimac, refusing to worship either. This had enraged Yegoth to a point that he had never before; it was the straw that broke the camel’s back. Both Morngdub and Yegoth screamed back and forth at each other with a rage that shook the mountains until both Morngdub and Shel were cast out from Clan Shatul, never to return or to contact anyone from the Clan. After a few days, they had both left, leaving Evnigdub to be stuck with his Father. At that point, the sentiments that Evnigdub had with his Father only increased until he became nothing more than an obedient Orc.  


  Nearly 10 years had passed since that point, each of them continuing to grow up in completely different environments. Morngdub and Shel, her mother, went off and wandered Orsinium for nearly half of that time; they were turned away almost everywhere they went. They mostly lived off the land and on the rare opportunities where someone showed them kindness. Morngdub wanted to leave Orsinium altogether; however, Shel couldn’t bear to leave her homeland. After a particularly bad winter, the two were at their wits' end; they were starving with nowhere left to go for shelter. One day, early in the morning, Shel woke Morngdub, saying that she had been given a vision that night and they needed to go high into the mountains to understand the meaning of it. They almost perished from the journey, traveling higher and higher, until finally they reached an abandoned location known as Pariah’s Pinnacle. Shel finally explained that she had been given a vision to come here by someone called the Woodland Man and that they needed to get inside. They had to move aside a lot of broken-down stone to get in, but once there, they found a Watchling named Yurg waiting for them. She explained that this location was provided to them by Hermaeus Mora and will continue to be theirs if they dedicate themselves to his worship and bring other like-minded individuals here to spread his influence. They agreed and founded Clan Agalor and have been in Pariah’s Pinnacle ever since.


  On the other hand, Evnigdub stayed with Yegoth, his father, in Clan Shatul. Evnigdub’s more quiet nature allowed him to go along with the rest of the clan; he didn’t speak out, he simply did what he was told. It was very hard to determine what he was thinking at any time, as he didn’t express many emotions and mostly preferred being by himself with no one else around. Over the years, he learned to become extremely stealthy to avoid others, and whenever he was told to fight, he would never be very good at it. Yegoth was incredibly disappointed in the attitude of his son and tried to convince him to become the warrior that he had wanted for years. Each time, however, it never worked, and all the while Evnigdub continued this life, growing more and more discontent with how everything turned out. Eventually, his father gave up on him and started growing more and more distant from his son. Evnigdub eventually found some kind of solace in hunting, as he was alone most often, and it allowed him to practice even further his stealthy skills. Soon enough, he became one of Clan Shatul’s main hunters and spent his days out in the woods.


  One day, while Evnigdub was out hunting, he received a message from his sister saying that she needed help as their mother had come down with a life-threatening sickness. He wanted to go help his sister, but was terrified of what his father would do if he tried to help. As he was debating what to do, his father approached him and told him that he had set up an arranged marriage to another person in the clan. This absolutely broke Evnigdub to his core. The woman that he was arranged to marry wasn’t an awful person, but he knew that marrying her would finalize his discontent with his life and trap him here. He knew he had to get out, and so one night he grabbed anything he would need to survive in the wilderness and left Clan Shatul behind to meet his sister. Once he arrived at Pariah’s Pinnacle, it was an extremely tearful reunion as they were both glad to see each other again.
