backstory: Kushrakh gro-Morkul was born beneath dark, smoked skies in a
  stronghold that skims the edge of Wrothgar’s coast like a blade to a grinder.
  This stronghold belonged to Clan Morkul, where iron was forged just as much as
  warriors. A place where weakness was remembered far longer than failure ever
  could be. It goes without saying that the stronghold proved to be lively by
  day and by night. With forges keeping the soil warm, and burly warriors
  keeping the hold safe. In Orsimer culture, at least when it comes to
  strongholds… everyone is expected to contribute. Men, women, and children. The
  differentiation between genders or even age mattered seldom. Spare the
  extremely and exceptionally old and young. In which case, the young were
  trained and taught to soon contribute, and most of the old sought an honorable
  death, or lent service until their passing anyway. Kushrakh’s father, Ghathmuk
  gro-Mork, was a respected warrior among many within the clan, who had followed
  in his father’s footsteps before him as a clan warrior within Morkul
  Stronghold. Kushrakh’s grandfather, Borzug gro-Bagbuk, had passed long before
  his father came of even fighting age. Ghathmuk himself, along with many other
  Orcish warriors of the clan, defended the stronghold through countless raids
  and skirmishes. In turn, he has also taken part in counterattacks, raids, and
  skirmishes as well. Kushrakh’s mother, Atubrza gra-Dulakul, was a huntress,
  one of many, who would stalk the surrounding areas and even tread into the
  brutal mountains of Wrothgar, coming back with game that was sometimes four
  times her own size. Growing up, Kushrakh showed little patience for the more
  time-based things of Orc culture. He couldn’t get the hang of smithing as fast
  as the other Orsimer children usually did. He wasn’t as loud as the other
  children either. He was quiet, patient, and seclusive. His mother would scold
  him, calling him impatient and irrational. Until the children came of age to
  experience fighting themselves, which doesn’t take long at all… others proved
  to be brash, brutal, and fought with pride. Kushrakh watched, he learned, he
  decided. While others were focused on their next fight and bragging rights,
  Kushrakh spent most of his time interacting with older folk, especially older
  warriors of the clan. Learning, watching, and deciding. His father called and
  told him to quit procrastinating, tried to instill a deep fear that if he did
  not act… he was dead. No matter what the conversation is. His father would
  come to train him in the way of war, and his mother taught him the way of the
  hunt. His grandmother, Urzala gra-Ushbog would often say to him, “Steel that
  hardens too fast becomes brittle”. As she was a well-respected blacksmith and
  forge-mother of the stronghold, also one of many. Her words, though, cut like
  the blades she beat into shape. Such words would shape the kind of Orc,
  Kushrakh would come to be. Kushrakh soon would become a well-respected warrior
  of the clan himself; his tactics proved true in everything he encountered. He
  would defend countless Morkul caravans, deny and counter many raids and
  skirmishes, and hunted beast who were at common times five times his size.
  Despite his skill, despite his contributions, Kushrakh began to grow tired of
  the confined lifestyle that was and is Orcish stronghold livelihood. You grow,
  you kill, you guard, you reproduce, you build, kill some more, teach your
  family to do the same thing, and die. Kushrakh wanted more, felt more, desired
  and hungered for more. He wanted to test himself against the wider and larger
  world; he knew there was more to see and experience, and he wanted to
  challenge it all. See it all. It was not unbeknownst to Orsimer that their
  young sometimes leave home for some reason or another. So, Kushrakh prepared
  decisively to set out. Before he left, his mother crafted him parting gifts. A
  large, double-headed axe that features engravings and symbols of the clan, so
  that he would never forget where he came from, an all-white bear cub that she
  had decided to save on one of her hunts, and a map.  After which, he left for
  the rest of Tamriel. He saw many provinces, wrapping the terrain along the
  coastline into Rivenspire, crossing the land to Glenumbra, going down and
  around to Stormhaven, continuing to Bangkorai, and finding his way into
  Craglorn. It was in Craglorn where he ran into many walks of life. He met the
  Iron Orcs, and some… other folk in the area, whom he often doesn’t speak of
  his interaction with. He met Imperial Legionaries, who eyed him for his
  stature and wondered how he would do in their frontlines. That was, until the
  one night in Crossroads Tavern. Craglorn boasts a lively population, whether
  it be man, mer, or beast. Kushrakh didn’t mind; he would be open to
  challenging them all. One night, he was drinking in a tavern named “Crossroads
  Tavern”. It was here where a group of thugs in the area had stumbled in…
  already drunk, and had quickly spotted Kushrakh. It was then and there that he
  had become a target. His stature, weaponry, and still demeanor mattered
  nought. They spoke aloud, taunting and boasting, provoking the Orc. Kushrakh
  knew he wasn’t home and knew he couldn’t confine everyone everywhere to Orcish
  standards. Folks had already been eyes locked onto the event, the bard had
  stopped playing music, and the bartender stopped serving drinks. The thugs
  kept poking, harmlessly, that was… until they noticed no give from the Orc,
  and wanting a fight, had smacked Kushrakh’s drink from his hand.In that
  instant, Kushrakh let loose. He stood up, hands under the table, gripped it
  with full strength, lifted it from the ground, and smashed it along the thug’s
  face. He fell quickly and abruptly. The other thugs dashed forward, but were
  humiliated in turn. The Orc clotheslined the first one and kicked the chest of
  the second. The third looked at his two comrades who had just fallen, and the
  one lying unconscious from the weaponized table, and drew his blade. In turn,
  Kushrakh grabbed his axe. It was at this point that everyone had already
  backed away enough to not get caught in the middle of this, but could also
  continue viewing. Some fled the scene the moment blades were drawn, some stood
  frozen in shock, or fear. The last standing thug rushed Kushrakh, thrashing
  wildly with his blade. Kushrakh simply waited and watched. Once the thug came
  into a dangerous close range, he side-stepped suddenly, and spun methodically,
  swinging the great axe toward the knees of the man. He dismembered his leg,
  and the man dropped to the floor in an instant pool of blood. By this time,
  guards had started storming in and were pushing past people who were trying to
  escape. All they saw was at least four different men lying unconscious or
  dead, and one Orc with his axe. They called more guards, who were not far
  away, and Kushrakh was FAR outnumbered. Not that he would’ve fought anyway,
  and though he would rather give equal trade or succumb to a blood price, he
  gave in. In the cells, someone who had heard of his ferocity spoke to him. He
  cut him a deal… of sorts. Fight for freedom. This seemed fair enough to him,
  as the idea of imprisonment is foreign to him and his kind. But, like all
  things, the pendulum swings. Kushrakh was lied to; this was not only some
  contact for underground fighting but someone who ran a secretive slave rink.
  Kushrakh was faced with overwhelming numbers and power at all times, unable to
  make many other choices at this point. He was branded on his face, a set of
  chains that began to wrap along his face, which overlapped diagonally by three
  claw marks. He fought for the entertainment of audiences, earned coin for
  betters and stagers of all kinds, and boosted the reputation of Orcs in the
  pit as well as Morkul Stronghold in kind. Soon, word spread across the crowds,
  a rumor of a fighter so ferocious yet so patient, that his opponents falter to
  the stoicism far before the axe. Some swore they heard roars as the fight
  raged on, some rumor his left ear turns black when he is in the presence of a
  liar, there are whispers that his weapon glows in the heat of battle, some
  think he has made some kind of dark deal, and you can see it dance across the
  bodies of dead lined upon the field. Despite all the rumors, Kushrakh
  remains.Over the years, Kushrakh became known across fighting circles as one
  of the most dangerous pit fighters in the region. Nobles wagered fortunes on
  his matches. Mercenary captains sought him for dangerous contracts. Criminal
  syndicates tried to buy his loyalty. Yet despite the fame, Kushrakh never
  abandoned the values he learned in Morkul.He refused to kill helpless
  opponents. He despised fighters who relied on cruelty for spectacle. Between
  battles, he often repaired his own armor and weapons rather than paying
  smiths, a habit that amused some and impressed others… yet even still, arena
  life changed him.Years of bloodshed hardened his spirit and left him wrestling
  with what kind of Orc he had become. Some Orc traditionalists viewed pit
  fighting as dishonorable compared to serving the clan and the stronghold.
  Others admired him for carrying the strength of Morkul into foreign lands.
  Kushrakh himself remained uncertain.Others know not of his inner struggles.
  They simply cheer his name or gossip about his tales. He earned a name, the
  “Pit Wolf”. Which stuck. He never thrashed out and mindlessly attacked
  opponents; he never tried to overwhelm them with a random barrage of assaults.
  He watched, he acted and reacted, he baited and would feint attacks, he would
  circle opponents rather than running directly back or forward at all times,
  and he stalked their movements. He was mindful of his own footwork and guard,
  his stances and form, and would hit hard and fast when he decided to strike.
  He fought like a hunter cornering prey, which frightened people most. After he
  survived a three-day gauntlet in Wayrest, against a mix of prisoners,
  mercenaries, warriors, fighters, veterans, beats, man, and mer, he finally
  earned his freedom. Some know he was a slave fighter, but some think he was
  there just as willingly as he was forced. Since then, he has continued
  wandering. Fishing in various lands as an outlet, crafting in his spare time,
  mainly refining his own armor, and tending to his bear, “Urgamug”. Accepting
  contracts, offering his services as either warrior, hunter, or smith. In the
  most recent time, he’s taken up cooking and brewing, in any sense that entails
  it. Though, despite all this, he still struggles within himself. Caught
  between the Orc he is, and the fighter he has become. Fans and audiences know
  him as an arena champion; Orcs know him as Morkul’s son. Kushrakh, he is
  fighting to truly realize where he belongs.
