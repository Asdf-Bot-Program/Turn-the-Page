backstory: Reylin was born in the frozen southern reaches of Skyrim, raised
  within a clan of warriors whose veteran members would undertake a secret
  ritual that reshaped body and skill alike. The middle child of two siblings,
  he was the son of one of the twin lords who led the clan. He never knew his
  mother; his father spoke of her as an accomplished mage of the College of
  Winterhold, but he would never learn why she was gone. To his aunt, Reylin was
  merely another of her brother’s bastards—but like his other siblings, his
  father loved him no less for it, taking personal charge to ensure they were
  taught in reading and writing just as well as they were trained in their
  respective forms of combat. From childhood, Reylin was trained in arms,
  hunting, and smithing. learning to hunt across the cold forest of Falkreath
  and to maintain his own gear at a forge or on the road. He was taught the
  clan’s history and their devotion to Kyne and Shor, honoring the gods through
  the sap of the Eldergleam and the ancient Nordic arts of Clever Craft to
  strengthen themselves, so their deeds might earn them a place in Sovngarde.
  These tales forged Reylin’s dream of joining the honored blooded warriors of
  his clan and living up to his father’s legacy. But that dream would die at sea
  for a few months after his sixteenth name day, Reylin and his father would
  answer a call for aid from a trusted companion of his father, only to be
  betrayed in the Sea of Ghosts. Their ship was boarded and sacked by warriors
  bearing their clan’s sigil—defiled with the mark of Oblivion. Amid the chaos,
  Reylin watched his father being struck down by the very man he'd considered an
  uncle. And in a futile rage, Reylin attacked the man, easily beaten and cast
  into the freezing sea as the ship burned behind him, those cold, uncaring eyes
  gazing down at him being the last thing he saw before losing consciousness.
  But death would not find him that day. Fished out of the cold depths, he would
  awaken in chains, for four years Reylin would be imprisoned and subjected to a
  profane mockery of his clan’s rituals. Eldergleam sap, Daedric boons, and
  corrupted Clever Craft were forced upon him. He was made to slay a werewolf in
  their arena, harvesting the fallen beast's blood to be mixed with other
  volatile ingredients into the sap as an offering to hircine, binding him with
  the horned Lord's gifts of lycanthropy. Daedric-tainted Clever Craft runes
  were carved into his flesh and bone, marring his body with their corrupted
  magics. Upon their twisted rituals' completion, they had turned him into a
  tool of warfare. Drugging him with various potions to drive him into a bestial
  frenzy, unleashing him onto battlefields and arenas for their dark gods'
  entertainment. His escape would come only when his captors grew complacent,
  falling to his lycanthropic rage during a failed transport. Broken and
  scarred, Reylin would begin his journey home, eventually returning to reveal
  the truth of his father’s fate and the clan’s betrayal. Though believed, he
  was seen as tainted and an ill omen. Understanding the Disdain of his
  affliction, Reylin would choose exile. wandering Skyrim as a mercenary for a
  time before meeting and joining the companions, now fighting with the cursed
  strength and reckless fury of his affliction, sharing tales and drink where he
  can, honoring his father and clan in spite of the tainted blood that marks
  him, all the while, the Hunt Father's gaze still follows him with patient
  interest.
