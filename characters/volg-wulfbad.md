backstory: >-
  He was born in blood, his mother screaming herself to death as he clawed his
  way into the world. The midwives called it a curse. The neighbors called him a
  bad omen. By the time he could walk, they called him nothing at all. Volg
  learned early that the world doesn’t care if you live or die. He grew in the
  gutters and frozen alleys of Windhelm, fighting for scraps, for warmth, for
  breath. Every broken nose, every cracked rib hardened him. He had no purpose.
  Only anger. Only survival. 


  They found him mid-fight, bloody, feral, laughing through broken teeth. The Undaunted didn’t see a curse. They saw a warrior worth sharpening. They gave him steel, battle and something dangerously close to belonging. Volg carved through dungeons beside them: Dwemer ruins, Daedric pits, ancient crypts. He laughed. He drank. He fought beside men and women who trusted him at their backs. For the first time in his life… he had something to lose. 


  It wasn’t supposed to be different. Just another dungeon, another contract, another payday. Instead, it was a slaughter. Deep beneath the earth, they found him a towering, near-immortal werewolf known only as Vicious. Not a beast… but a king of beasts, starved, waiting. Then came the pack…It wasn’t a fight, it was a massacre. Claws tore through armor like parchment, jaws crushed bone like wood. The Undaunted screamed, fought, died. One by one, ripped apart in a frenzy of teeth and blood, limbs were scattered across stone and entrails painted the walls. The air filled with the wet, choking sound of men being eaten alive. Volg watched them fall, everyone… His family, his only family. When it was over, he was still standing, broken and bleeding out. Propped up only by his greatsword driven into the stone beneath him, then the werewolf came for him. The beast lunged, jaws wide, and Volg could not move. The bite crushed into his flesh, pain beyond pain. A sickness forced into his blood, but as the beast tore into him… it faltered. The great wolf’s strength gave out. Its ancient body, spent from the slaughter, collapsed even as its curse took root inside Volg. It died at his feet, but it did not leave him. 


  Volg rose again. He should have died in that dungeon, instead, he crawled out of it, reborn in something worse than life. The curse was not a gift, it was a cancer. Lycanthropy burned through him like a disease, granting monstrous strength, sharpened senses, endurance that refused death…at the cost of everything that made him human, he became a wandering blade across Tamriel. Like some twisted echo of The Undaunted he used to be, Volg hunted monsters, daedra, anything that paid, throwing himself into the worst hells he could find. For coin, for distraction. Because sometimes… he lost it. Sometimes the wolf took him and when it did, there was no difference between innocent and guilty. Only meat.


  His path dragged him into the Reach, toward Markarth, where the Grey Host stirred once more. He cut his way through vampires, monsters, and men alike, leaving a trail of corpses across the wilds, until his body finally failed him. Half-dead, delirious, more beast than man, he was found by reach witches, they studied him. What they found was worse than any curse. Not lycanthropy but something deeper, A cancer of the soul: The more blood Volg spilled, the stronger the wolf became. One day they warned him, the beast, Vicious, would fully devour him. No Afterlife, no Sovngarde, Just oblivion… or worse. They gave him a remedy. A thick, black concoction, tar-like, bitter, foul. Not a cure, A leash, It dulled the wolf and bought him time. But time was all it could ever buy. 


  Volg drifted to a place called Sugar Falls, chasing coin tied to Daedric activity and missing arcanists. He expected another job,Instead, he found something far more dangerous. 


  People. 


  Evening, an Orsimer ranger, who treated him like a man, not a monster, and proved connection wasn’t weakness. 


  Mia Roseblood, a sharp-tongued Bosmer vampire assassin, quick with a blade, quicker with a joke, who saved his life more times than he would ever admit. 


  Vivienne Morningstar, an ancient vampire matron whose discipline and iron-willed kindness forced Volg to confront the beast within. 


  For a moment, Volg wasn’t alone. 


  On the road again, Volg was taken. Captured by a blood mage named Caedus.He didn’t kill Volg…Through ritual and torment, Caedus forced the wolf and the man closer together, binding them, sharpening them, turning Volg into something else. Volg was unleashed upon a village. 


  And he slaughtered it. 


  Men, Women, Children, all Torn apart to fuel the mage’s power. When it was done, when Volg had served his purpose, Caedus discarded him like a dull blade. 


  Dying, Rotting, Losing himself. Volg crawled back to the only place his instincts called safe, Sugar Falls. They saved him again, Mia, Evening, and Vivienne, They brought him back from the brink of death. But something had changed, the wolf wasn’t just a beast anymore. It was him. 


  Now Volg walks Tamriel again, blade in hand. But the fight is no longer simple, It is not man vs monster, It is two survivors… trapped in the same body.Volg And Vicious. Circling each other, testing each other, Refusing to die. He hunts monsters, But he knows the truth. One day…he will lose and when he does, he won’t just die. He’ll become the very thing he’s spent his life slaughtering.
